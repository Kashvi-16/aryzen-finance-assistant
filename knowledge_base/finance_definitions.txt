
# Financial Glossary – High-Level, Safe, Non-Advisory

1. Asset: Anything with economic value that can be owned or controlled.
2. Liability: A financial obligation or debt owed to another party.
3. Equity: Ownership interest in a company represented by shares.
4. Capital: Money or assets used to invest or grow a business.
5. Investment: Allocation of money into assets expecting future returns.
6. Return: The gain or loss on an investment.
7. Risk: The chance that actual returns differ from expected returns.
8. Diversification: Reducing risk by spreading investments across assets.
9. Portfolio: A collection of investments owned by an individual or firm.
10. Asset Allocation: The distribution of investments across asset classes.
11. Mutual Fund: A pooled investment vehicle holding diversified assets.
12. ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund): A fund that trades like a stock but holds a basket of assets.
13. Hedge Fund: Privately managed fund using advanced investment strategies.
14. Private Equity: Investment in private companies or buyouts of public ones.
15. Venture Capital: Funding provided to early-stage high-growth companies.
16. Fixed Income: Investments providing regular interest payments.
17. Bonds: Debt securities issued by governments or corporations.
18. Treasury Bills: Short-term government debt instruments.
19. Corporate Bonds: Bonds issued by companies to raise capital.
20. Government Securities: Debt issued by a national government.
21. Yield: The income return on an investment, usually expressed annually.
22. Compounding: Earning returns on previously earned returns.
23. NAV (Net Asset Value): Value per unit of a fund.
24. AUM (Assets Under Management): Total market value managed by an institution.
25. Market Capitalization: Total value of a company’s outstanding shares.
26. Blue-Chip Stocks: Shares of large and financially stable companies.
27. Small-Cap Stocks: Shares of smaller companies with growth potential.
28. Mid-Cap Stocks: Stocks of medium-sized companies.
29. Large-Cap Stocks: Stocks of major corporations.
30. Dividend: Portion of company profits paid to shareholders.
31. Earnings Per Share (EPS): Company profit divided by outstanding shares.
32. P/E Ratio: Price divided by earnings per share.
33. Valuation: Process of determining an asset’s worth.
34. Liquidity: How quickly an asset can be converted into cash.
35. Inflation: Rise in general price levels over time.
36. Deflation: Decrease in price levels.
37. Interest Rate: Cost of borrowing money.
38. Repo Rate: Rate at which central banks lend to commercial banks.
39. Reverse Repo Rate: Rate at which banks deposit money with central banks.
40. Monetary Policy: Central bank actions controlling money supply and rates.
41. Fiscal Policy: Government decisions on spending and taxation.
42. GDP (Gross Domestic Product): Total value of goods and services produced.
43. CPI (Consumer Price Index): Measure of inflation based on consumer prices.
44. Market Index: Benchmark representing market performance.
45. Sensex: Indian stock market index of 30 major companies.
46. Nifty 50: Index of 50 Indian blue-chip companies.
47. Stock Exchange: Marketplace for buying and selling securities.
48. Primary Market: Where new securities are issued.
49. Secondary Market: Where investors trade existing securities.
50. IPO (Initial Public Offering): When a company sells shares to public for first time.
51. FPO: Follow-on Public Offer after IPO.
52. Buyback: When a company repurchases its own shares.
53. Market Volatility: Degree of price fluctuation in markets.
54. Bull Market: Period of rising asset prices.
55. Bear Market: Period of declining asset prices.
56. Stop-Loss: Predefined price at which an investor exits a trade.
57. Asset Management Company (AMC): Manages investments for clients.
58. Wealth Management: Advisory and investment services for individuals.
59. Financial Planning: Organizing finances to meet long-term goals.
60. Fund Manager: Professional responsible for managing investment funds.
61. Expense Ratio: Annual cost of managing a fund.
62. Sharpe Ratio: Reward-to-risk measure for investments.
63. Beta: Measure of a stock’s volatility relative to the market.
64. Alpha: Excess return above benchmark.
65. Risk Profile: An investor’s comfort level with risk.
66. Credit Score: Rating representing an individual's creditworthiness.
67. Collateral: Asset pledged to secure a loan.
68. Loan-to-Value Ratio: Measure comparing loan amount to asset value.
69. Fixed Deposit (FD): Fixed-return savings instrument offered by banks.
70. Recurring Deposit (RD): Deposit where money is added monthly.
71. Savings Account: Bank account for basic savings and interest.
72. Current Account: Account for business transactions.
73. Credit Card: Card allowing purchases on borrowed money.
74. Debit Card: Card enabling spending from bank balance.
75. EMI (Equated Monthly Installment): Monthly loan repayment.
76. Mortgage: Loan used to buy property.
77. Real Estate Investment Trust (REIT): Company owning or financing real estate.
78. Commodities: Physical goods like gold, oil, or grains.
79. Derivatives: Financial contracts based on underlying assets.
80. Futures: Contract to buy or sell an asset at a future date.
81. Options: Contracts giving the right but not obligation to buy or sell.
82. Margin: Borrowed money used to trade financial instruments.
83. Leverage: Using debt to increase potential returns.
84. Short Selling: Selling borrowed shares hoping to buy them back cheaper.
85. Inflation Hedge: Asset used to protect against rising prices.
86. Safe Haven Asset: Asset that retains value in market stress (e.g., gold).
87. Asset Bubble: When asset prices rise far above fundamental value.
88. Credit Risk: Risk of borrower defaulting.
89. Market Risk: Risk due to market fluctuations.
90. Operational Risk: Risk from failed systems or processes.
91. Systematic Risk: Market-wide risk.
92. Unsystematic Risk: Company-specific risk.
93. Benchmark: Reference index used to measure performance.
94. Time Horizon: Duration an investor plans to stay invested.
95. SIP (Systematic Investment Plan): Periodic mutual fund investment method.
96. Lump Sum Investment: One-time investment amount.
97. KYC: Verification process to confirm identity.
98. Nominee: Individual who receives assets upon the investor’s death.
99. Trustee: Person or company managing assets on behalf of another.
100. Fund House: Institution managing multiple investment funds.
101. Custodian: Entity safeguarding financial assets.
102. Clearing House: Facilitates settlement of trades.
103. Brokerage: Firm enabling buying/selling of securities.
104. Brokerage Fee: Charge for executing trades.
105. Market Order: Order to buy/sell immediately at best price.
106. Limit Order: Order to trade at a preset price.
107. Volatility Index (VIX): Measure of expected market volatility.
108. Inflation-Indexed Bonds: Bonds protected against inflation.
109. Credit Rating: Evaluation of creditworthiness of issuer.
110. Discount Rate: Rate used to calculate present value of future cash flows.
111. Capital Gains: Profit from selling an asset.
112. Holding Period: Duration an investment is held.
113. Asset Rebalancing: Adjusting portfolio to target allocation.
114. ESG Investing: Considering environmental, social, and governance factors.
115. Fund Benchmarking: Comparing fund performance against a standard.
116. Financial Literacy: Understanding basic financial concepts.
117. Working Capital: Money needed for day-to-day operations.
118. Cash Flow: Amount of cash coming in and going out.
119. Balance Sheet: Statement showing assets, liabilities, and equity.
120. Income Statement: Statement showing revenue and profit of a company.
